Filling systems

2020-07-302020-10-14 HgXzMnFountain Pen 101

There are many filling systems in fountain pens. The filling system is important as it allows ink to easily flow from the ink reservoir to the nib of the pen. Different mechanisms allow different capacities for ink and different levels of convenience. Here are a few common ones:

  • Cartridge Converter (or c/c). This is the most common and often most convenient filling system. A small container of ink that contains around one ml of ink is directly plugged into the feed. This container is the ink reservoir, and is disposed when empty, replaced with a new one. The converter part comes when you want to use ink from a bottle of ink. A converter is basically a small piston filler that is often a screw piston though also sometimes a push piston.
  • Piston Filler. Piston fillers are often the most regularly used premium filling system. They are very versatile as they can store a large amount of ink and is easy to use. A piston filler is often a screw that pushes a piston to suck up ink with the tension.
  • Push Piston. Push pistons are rather rare as the amount of ink that is able to be stored is cut in half because of the mechanism it uses. They are rare and most of the time they are found in Converters, not an actual mechanism like a piston filler
  • Vacuum filler. A vacuum filler is one of the rarer filling systems. It is easy to use and have a large capacity, but are often misused. They are rare and cannot easily be found. The fountain pen pictured to the left is a Pilot Custom 823.
  • Sac fillers. Sac fillers are a huge category of filling systems that are generally relate with vintage fountain pens. They include crescent, lever, pneumatic and many more. True to their name, they involve squeezing a sack to suck ink
